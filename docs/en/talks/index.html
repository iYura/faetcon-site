Talks — Call for Proposals

FAETCon is looking for speakers passionate about electronics, avionics, embedded systems, robotics, RF hacking, badge hacking, and related fields. Don't limit yourself — we love unexpected projects too.

Who can apply

Electronic hardware designers
Mechanical hardware designers
Electronics enthusiasts
Firmware/embedded
Software/web/app
Makers
RF/radio amateurs
UI/graphics designers

Formats

  • 20 minutes — a short talk, great for a first-time speaker.
  • 45 minutes — a longer talk with room for detail.
  • Workshop (1–2 hrs) — hands-on session; needs roughly one helper per 10 attendees.

Example talks

To show the level and format we have in mind, here are selections from the two conferences we look to: Hackaday Supercon and the hardware track of the Chaos Communication Congress.

This is not the FAETCon program and these are not our speakers — they spoke at Supercon in Pasadena and at CCC in Hamburg. We list them as a reference for topics and delivery.

What to include in your proposal

  • Name, contact info, a short 3–5 sentence bio (links to projects/socials welcome).
  • Talk/workshop title and description — what you'll cover and why it matters.
  • Format and preferred length.
  • Target audience and 2–3 things attendees will learn.
  • For workshops — whether you'll need a helper and an estimated material cost per person.
  • Whether this is your first time speaking (we deliberately keep room for first-timers).
